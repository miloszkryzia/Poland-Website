Poland's Biggest Cities

Warsaw (Warszawa)

Warsaw is Poland's capital and largest city.

It is also the 7th largest city in the European Union

Warsaw is a major tourist destination and cultural center in Europe.

Cracow (Kraków)

Cracow is one of Poland's oldest cities.

It used to be its capital.

Cracow is another major tourist destinantion in Poland

and it has traditionally been the country's leading

academic, cultural and artistic life.

Tricity (Trójmiasto)

The Tricity actually consists of three major cities:

Gdansk, Gdynia and Sopot.

It's an important center of education and

another popular destination for tourists.

Katowice

Katowice is a city in Southern Poland.

It's a part of a large metropolis which also includes

My hometown, Dąbrowa Górnicza.

The city itself and surrounding area is a major industrial center

of the country. The city also serves as a host of many eSports events.

The biggest two are the ESL Katowice Tournament and

Intel Extreme Masters World Championship, one of the biggest events of this kind in the world.
